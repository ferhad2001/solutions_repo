âš¡ Lorentz Force

The Lorentz force on a charged particle is given by:

\[ \vec{F} = q(\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \]

Where: - \( \vec{F} \): Force on the particle (N) - \( q \): Charge of the particle (C) - \( \vec{E} \): Electric field (V/m) - \( \vec{v} \): Particle velocity (m/s) - \( \vec{B} \): Magnetic field (T)

ğŸ§® Equations of Motion

From Newton's second law:

\[ \vec{F} = m \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} = \frac{q}{m} (\vec{E} + \vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \]
\[ \frac{d\vec{r}}{dt} = \vec{v} \]

These are solved numerically using Euler or Runge-Kutta methods.

ğŸ”„ Euler Method (Discretized Form)

\[ \vec{v}_{n+1} = \vec{v}_n + \frac{q}{m} (\vec{E} + \vec{v}_n \times \vec{B}) \Delta t \]
\[ \vec{r}_{n+1} = \vec{r}_n + \vec{v}_n \Delta t \]

ğŸ” Runge-Kutta Method (4th Order â€” Conceptual)

Intermediate steps:

\[ k_1 = f(t_n, y_n) \quad k_2 = f\left(t_n + \frac{h}{2}, y_n + \frac{h}{2}k_1\right) \quad k_3 = f\left(t_n + \frac{h}{2}, y_n + \frac{h}{2}k_2\right) \quad k_4 = f\left(t_n + h, y_n + hk_3\right) \]

Then update:

\[ y_{n+1} = y_n + \frac{h}{6}(k_1 + 2k_2 + 2k_3 + k_4) \]

ğŸ” Larmor Radius (for Circular Motion in Magnetic Field)

\[ r_L = \frac{mv_\perp}{|q|B} \]

Where \( v_\perp \) is the component of velocity perpendicular to \( \vec{B} \).

Where: - \( \omega_c \): Angular frequency of circular motion - \( T \): Period of revolution
